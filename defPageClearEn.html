Ariane de Rothschild Woman Doctoral program 2020-21

 

About the program

The Ariane de Rothschild Women's Doctoral Program grants scholarships to outstanding female PhD students from various fields of study, who study at research institutions in Israel. As part of the Baroness's efforts to promote equal opportunity for women around the world, the program supports talented and motivated female doctoral students from diverse backgrounds, whose scholarship is a necessary condition for their success in academia.

 

Eligibility

Excellent female PhD students who are Israeli residents and have Israeli citizenship, registered for doctoral studies in current academic year 2019-2020 (no earlier than October 2019).

Screening includes examining candidates' compliance with the criteria of academic excellence (50%), and a personal background that takes into consideration social diversity, leadership, social involvement, and socio-economic needs (50%).

Scholarship information

The scholarship, 60,000 NIS is provided for up to four years, and is contingent upon the student's progress in studies and research. In the second year, the student will receive a grant of 20,000 NIS to present research at international conferences abroad.

The Hebrew University will match the scholarship, and provide an additional sum of 40,000 NIS per year and 160,000 NIS in total for the four years of the program.

Students who receive the scholarships are committed to 56 hours of volunteering in educational projects each year, thus empowering young people and serving as role models, excellence and giving to the community.

Application process

  1. Complete the online application form
  2. Upload all the following documents, in English to your personal documents folder according to these guidelines :
    • CV – First page should include the following personal information: First and last name, ID number (teudat zehut), Date of birth, Place of birth, Aliyah year, address, mobile phone number, Faculty, Department, Research topic, Name of PhD instructor
    • List of Publications (including Impact Factor & Impact Rank) , include also attended conferences and list of prizes and awards
    • Grade transcripts (Higher education only, in English) 
    • Personal letter to scholarship committee - including description of volunteer work and social involvement (1-2 pages)
    • Research summary (1-2 pages)
    • Commitment form – see page 3 of Call for Nominations – original document must be signed and uploaded
  1. Two letters of recommendation, one from present PhD supervisor (in the case of two PhD supervisors three letters should be provided) - should be uploaded according to these guidelines
  2. To complete the submission process go to the Submission Status page and follow the guidelines
