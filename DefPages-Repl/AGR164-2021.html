Appleby Canada Scholarships for Outstanding MSc Students – 2021-2022

The Robert H. Smith Faculty of Agriculture, Food and Environment and the Keren Kayemet Le-Israel (KKL, the Jewish National Fund) announce the scholarship program for the academic year 2021-2022, funded by the KKL, for outstanding MSc students. The program aims to encourage outstanding students to study and excel in topics related to afforestation, land management and nature conservation.

MSc students studying in the Faculty of Agriculture, with a BSc grade average of no less than 85, and whose thesis concerns afforestation or land management, may apply for the scholarship. The scholarship is 12,000 NIS. The recipients will be selected from among 1st or 2nd year candidates. The application will be accompanied by a letter from the candidate's supervisor providing a brief description of the research objectives and confirming that the research concerns afforestation or land management.

2nd year MSc students will have to reach a 1st year MSc grade average of no less than 85.

Preference will be given to students whose research topics concerns (by descending order): (1) forest management, (2) forestry without management aspects or management of natural woodland and shrubland, (3) natural woodland and shrubland without management aspects (4) other natural ecosystems and landscapes. Preference will also be given to students with exceptionally high BSc grades.

The recipients will be required to attend several events:

  • The Appleby Scholarship awards ceremony in the Faculty of Agriculture
  • The KKL annual scholarship awards ceremony
  • Exposition day to the work done in KKL and to forest and land management in Israel.

Students may apply even if they receive other scholarships or awards, provided that the overall sum of their scholarships is no higher than 340%.

Application deadline: November 28, 2021

Application procedure

  1. Fill out the online application form.
  2. Upload the following documents to the Personal Document Upload File according to Documents Upload page
    • Confirmation of enrollment for 2021-2022
    • CV, concisely written according to Documents Upload page/font>
    • List of publications, if relevant, written according to  Documents Upload page/font>
    • Thesis topic and abstract
    • BSc and MSc records of studies
    • MSc study program (courses)
    • Research plan (for 2nd year students)
  3. To complete the process, please follow the instructions in the Application Submittal Status.

 

Please verify that the online form is fully and precisely filled out. Applications submitted without a fully completed online form will be disqualified. Applications submitted without all the necessary data (recommendations or any other required documents) will be disqualified. All the required documents must be uploaded until the application deadline. Documents submitted after the deadline will not be considered.
