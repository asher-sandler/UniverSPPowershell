Polonsky Prizes for Creativity and Originality

in the Humanities - 2022


The Authority for Research and Development and the Faculty of Humanities at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem are pleased to announce the Polonsky Prizes for Creativity and Originality in the Humanities for the year 2022.

The Polonsky Prizes were initiated in 2005 by Dr. Leonard Polonsky, a prominent member of the British Friends of The Hebrew University, who has very generously set up this award to provide support for research in the Humanities.

The prizes are intended for nominees belonging to the Faculty of Humanities* at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, in the following three categories:

A.   Researchers (for a book or an article published within the last two years)
 
B.   Post-Doctoral researchers (for their dissertation, accepted within the last three years)
 
C.   Ph.D. or M.A. students (for a published article, or for an M.A. thesis accepted within the past year)

 

Up to four prizes – two researcher prizes, one post-doctorate and one student prize – will be awarded, in person only, in the ceremony at the University Board of Governors annual meeting, to be held between June 10-15, 2022. Prizes shall range from $2,000–$5,000 for PhD and M.A. students and $5,000–$6,000 for post-doctoral researchers and $7,500 – $12,500 (pre-tax) for researchers.

Both self-nominations and nominations by colleagues and students may be submitted. The works submitted will be judged for creativity and originality.

The last date to submit applications is: 06.01.2022
 

Application Submission

A. Fill out the Online Application Form
B. Upload the following documents according to the upload instructions:

  • An abstract of 1-2 pages, in English and in Hebrew, explaining the creativity and originality of the work

  • 2-3 colorful illustrations or photos reflecting your research (in jpeg format, in high quality)

For Category A: Researchers:

Two letters of recommendation from scholars, in English or Hebrew, from within or outside The Hebrew University - sent according to the guidelines described here

  • In the case of books, 1-2 sample chapters, up to 50 pages

If the submission is for a book, in addition to the on-line submission, 2 hard copies of the book should be submitted to Shulamith Lasnes at the Faculty of Humanities, room no. 4502 (Sundays and Mondays between 10:00 – 14:00)

For Category B & C: Post-Doctoral researchers, Ph.D. and M.A. students:

  • Post-doctoral researchers ONLY: the evaluations of the dissertation

C. Two letters of recommendation from scholars, in English or Hebrew, from within or outside The Hebrew University - sent according to the guidelines described here
D. Article/Chapter up to 30 pages or the full thesis
E. Please follow the instructions shown on the 'Submission Status' page
 

Contact Person at the Faculty of Humanities:

Shulamith Lasnes - Coordinator, Committee for Scholarships at the Faculty of Humanities, 02-5883851 (Mondays and Wednesdays, 10:00-14:00 Room 4502 Humanities), Shulamitl@savion.huji.ac.il
 
* Candidates from other Faculties at the Hebrew University who believe that their work is relevant to the Humanities should inquire about possible exception with the Vice-Dean of Research at the Faculty of Humanities, Professor Yossi Maurey,  e-mail address: yossi.maurey@mail.huji.ac.il
 

Office of the Vice President for Research & Development, Edmond J Safra Campus

Tel: 972-206586626 Fax: 972-2-6529764, E-Mail: eldadj@savion.huji.ac.il
