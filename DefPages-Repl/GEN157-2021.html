Scholarship​s for Doctoral Students 

at the Truman School for Peace Studies, the Harry S. Truman Research Institute for the Advancement of ​Peace at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem

 

Eligibility:

Students who will enrol as first-year doctoral students in the 2021/2022 academic year at any of the departments of the Hebrew University of Jerusalem are hereby invited to submit their application to the Truman School for Peace Studies at the Truman Institute. 

Doctoral research must be related to the study of peace and conflict resolution, with an emphasis on one or all of the following topics: the Middle East; the Israeli-Palestinian conflict; Jerusalem. 

Admission and Terms:

Admission to the school is highly competitive, and an academic committee will select the best candidates. Doctoral students selected to the program will receive a $17,000 fellowship each year for four years. Transition from one year to the next is conditional upon the approval of the Academic Committee of the Institute which will examine the progress of​​ the research and the student's conduct in the program. There will also be an additional budget "package" available, which will include, for example, assistance in editing articles in English, travel abroad for research purposes, and travel to international conferences.  Accepted students will be given office space at the Truman Institute.

As a condition of the scholarship, students must be present at the Truman Institute at least three days per week. In addition, the program will require registration for intensive courses on peace research and other relevant issues. The courses will be given by guest lecturers. Each course will be worth 1-2 credit points, and 4 credit points must be accumulated during studies at the Institute. Students at the Truman School will participate in a doctoral seminar on the topic of peace research and the advancement of peace. Students will organize conferences and workshops in the subject, and they are expected to be active participants in the organization of the Truman's Institute's annual international conference whose theme changes each year.

The aim of this scholarship program is to create a community of outstanding doctoral students in the field of peace research and conflict resolution, and to enhance thinking in and around the field, while providing them an academic support and an opportunity to focus on their research. 

 
Closing date for applications: January 31, 2021
 

​Submission Procedure:​

A. Complete and save the online applic​ation form (personal information questionnaire)
B. Upload the following documents to your personal folder (see instructions for uploading):

  • A cover letter explaining how the proposed study relates to the aims of the Truman Institute. (The website of the Institute can provide more information about these aims.)

  • A short document (Word, up to three page, in English, Times New Roman 12 font, 1.5 line spacing) giving more detailed explanation regarding the proposed research.

  • A letter of approval in principle from a faculty member at the Hebrew University to supervise the doctorate.​

  • Academic transcripts of grades from all institutions of higher education attended.

 
C. Two letters of recommendation - should be sent directly to the email address provided by the system (see instructions)
  
D. Please follow the instructions shown on 'Submission Status' page
 

Rights and Duties:

Successful applicants will be required to sign a form confirming their agreement to the rights and duties of Truman Institute Fellows.

  1. To submit a short scientific progress report and an updated list of publications and presentations at the end of the fellowship year.

  2. In cases where the fellowship is for a period longer than a year, the fellowship can be renewed each year only with the approval of the Academic Committee; a detailed research report and a letter from the dissertation advisor or faculty host must be submitted to the Academic Committee upon request.

  3. To mention the affiliation with the Truman Institute in every printed or digital publication that was prepared, partly or fully, during the research year (even if it was sent for publication after the research year).

  4. To mention the Truman Institute as your affiliation in all conferences, here and abroad, and in all interviews and publications in the media.

  5. To be present at the Institute at least three days per week in the course of the school year, other than planned absences of which the management of the Institute should be informed in advance.

  6. Participation in all fellow-oriented Truman activities. In addition, active participation in the ongoing academic activity of the Institute, including conferences, seminars, etc., is encouraged. Doctoral students and postdocs are expected to initiate and organize academic activities during their stay at the institute.

  7. The fellowships come with restrictions on the number of hours which can be worked in paid employment, as specified in the rules of HU's Academic Secretariat.

  8. To participate and assist in hosting and in meetings with guests of the Institute from Israel and abroad.

  9. To lecture on the subject of your research in various forums that the Institute shall organize.

  10. Fellows are entitled to photocopying and printing services at the Institute, up to 1,000 pages per year (we will appreciate efforts to use less paper as part of overall environmental awareness).

  11. Fellows will be given office space at the Institute which they will share with other fellows.

  12. Fellows are entitled to use, when possible, the event halls at the Institute.

  13. The facilities and services of the Institute shall not be used other than according to the terms and conditions established in advance.

  14. The Academic Committee is authorized to terminate the activity of a fellow at its discretion.

For further information:

Please email the Truman Institute at truman@savion.huji.ac.il or call 02-588-2300 (international 972-2-5882300).​
