Scholarship​s for Doctoral Students 

between one and four years

at the Truman School for Peace Studies, the Harry S. Truman Research Institute for the Advancement of ​Peace at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem

 

 

The scholarship is intended for doctoral students enrolled at the Hebrew University who are in various stages of the doctorate and those who will enroll in the year 2022-23.  The length of the scholarship will be determined, among other things, by the stage of the doctorate and no later than the fourth year of its commencement when applying for the scholarship.

 

Doctoral research must be related to the study of peace and conflict resolution, with an emphasis on one or all of the following topics: the Middle East; the Israeli-Palestinian conflict; Jerusalem. 

Admission and Terms:

Admission to the school is highly competitive, and an academic committee will select the best candidates. Doctoral students selected to the program will receive a 60,000 shekel fellowship each year for the duration of the scholarship. Transition from one year to the next is conditional upon the approval of the Academic Committee of the Institute which will examine the progress of the research and the student's conduct in the program. There will also be an additional financial "package" available, which will include, for example, assistance in editing articles in English, travel abroad for research purposes, and travel to international conferences.  Accepted students will be given office space at the Truman Institute.

As a condition of the scholarship, students must be present at the Truman Institute at least three days per week. The Institute will offer relevant courses to the doctoral fellows.  In addition, students at the Truman School will participate in a doctoral seminar on the topic of peace research and the advancement of peace. Students will be expected to be active participants in the organization of the Truman's Institute's conferences, workshops and events.

 

The aim of this scholarship program is to continue the development of an outstanding community of doctoral students engaged in peace research and conflict resolution, and to encourage thinking in the field, while providing them academic support and an opportunity to focus on their research.


One scholarship will be awarded in the name of Yehoshua and Ruth Gilutz z”l.

 
Closing date for applications: January 9, 2022
 

​Submission Procedure:​

A. Complete and save the online applic​ation form (personal information questionnaire)
B. Upload the following documents to your personal folder (see Documents Upload page):

  • CV (including the date the doctoral studies began and the date of approval of stage B, if relevant).

  • Cover letter.

  • Research proposal (Word, up to three pages, in English, Times New Roman 12 font, 1.5 line spacing).

  • Academic transcripts of grades from all institutions of higher education.

  • ** Students not currently enrolled at the Hebrew University should also attach an endorsement from a supervisor at the Hebrew University.​

  • ** Students at an advanced stage of the doctoral studies should attach the table of contents of the dissertation and a report from the supervisor on the stage of the dissertation.

 
C. Two letters of recommendation - should be sent directly to the email address provided by the system (see instructions)
  
D. Please follow the instructions shown on 'Submission Status' page
 

Rights and Duties:

Successful applicants will be required to sign a form confirming their agreement to the rights and duties of Truman Institute Fellows.

I undertake the following:

1.              To do to my utmost in order to complete my doctoral dissertation during the period of eligibility of the Truman Institute Scholarship, as it appears in the scholarship confirmation letter.

2.              Not to work in any paid employment exceeding 50% of a full-time position during the period of eligibility. I undertake to notify the Truman Institute management if I receive additional scholarships, and will follow the procedures that appear on the website of the Academic Administration Division of the University.

3.              I know that in the event that I cease my research, my eligibility for a scholarship will cease.

4.              I am aware that the Truman Institute is entitled to stop payment of the scholarship at any time if, at the discretion of the Institute's Academic Committee, progress in research work is unsatisfactory, or academic achievement does not justify continued support, or that the terms of the scholarship have been breached. This is after I have been given the opportunity to make my claims in writing or orally. This decision can be appealed to the Vice-Rector.

5.              I  will be required to submit a short scientific progress report and an updated list of publications and presentations at the end of the scholarship year.

6.              In cases where the scholarship is for a period of one year with an option for additional years, renewal of the scholarship is at the discretion of the Academic Committee at the end of each year. A detailed research report from the fellow and a letter from the dissertation advisor or faculty host must be submitted to the Academic Committee upon request.

7.              The approval of the head of the institute must be requested regarding planned expenses for research/editing/travel to a conference or archival research and any other related research expenses for which the scholarship holder requests the financial assistance of the Institute. These expenses will not be recognized if they are not requested in advance.

8.              I am aware that in cases where it is necessary to travel overseas for a long period (a semester or longer) during the scholarship period, prior approval must be requested from the Academic Committee. The application will include:

·                An explanation of the importance and the contribution of a stay abroad to the research;

·                Approval of the advisor, including an explanation of how the relationship with the student will be maintained during the trip;

·                Confirmation from the host institution or from the host researcher (if there is a formal host).

The Scholarship Committee of the Institute will consider the application but does not commit to approve it. As a general rule, travel for a period more than 12 months will not be approved.

9.              I undertake that most of my academic activities will be at the Truman Institute on the university campus. I am aware that I must obtain prior approval from the Academic Committee for ongoing academic activity outside the University.

10.          To mention the affiliation with the Truman Institute in every printed or digital publication that was prepared, in part or in full, during the research period funded by the Truman Institute (even if it was sent for publication after the research period).

11.          To mention the Truman Institute as my affiliation in all conferences, in Israel and abroad, and in all interviews and publications in the media.

12.          To be present at the Institute at least three days per week in the course of the school year, not taking into consideration planned absences of which the management of the Institute must be informed in advance.

13.          To participate in all fellow-oriented Truman activities. In addition, active participation in the ongoing academic activities of the Institute, including conferences, seminars, etc. Doctoral students and post-doctoral students are expected to initiate and organize academic activities during their stay at the institute.

14.          To participate and assist in hosting and meetings with the guests of the Institute from Israel and abroad.

15.          To lecture on the subject of your research in various forums that the Institute shall organize.

16.          Fellows are entitled to photocopying and printing services at the Institute, up to 1,000 pages per year (we will appreciate efforts to use less paper as part of overall environmental awareness).

17.          Fellows will be given office space at the Institute which they will share with other fellows.

18.          Fellows are entitled to use, when available, the event halls at the Institute.

19.          The facilities and services of the Institute shall not be used other than according to the terms and conditions established in advance.


For further information:

Please email the Truman Institute at truman@savion.huji.ac.il or call 02-588-2300 (international 972-2-5882300).​
