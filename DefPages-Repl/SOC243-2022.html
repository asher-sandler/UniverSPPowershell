Support for Canadian Speakers  
Online Program 2023-2024

 

The Halbert Centre for Canadian Studies can assist in funding online talks by Canadian speakers at the Hebrew University on Canada related topics.

Detailed requests, including the event's program, must be submitted to the Halbert Centre for Canadian Studies by February 12, 2023

 For further details please visit the Halbert Centre Website

This program includes two categories:

  1. Funding of up to 500 CD will be granted for a Canadian Speaker giving a lecture within a conference or departmental seminar.
  2. Funding of up to 1,000 CD will be granted for a Canadian Speaker giving a day-long seminar.

Utilization of the grant is valid from October 2023 until September 2024.
Applicants for the grant will be informed of the approval or rejection of their requests by the end of March 2022.
The budget will be managed by the Halbert Center.

*Final approval of the applications is subject to budget confirmation from the Hebrew University.

Submission deadline: 12.02.2023

Application process

  1. Complete the online application form
  2. B. The following documents should be uploaded via the Documents Upload page
    • CV – A short CV of the Canadian speaker.
    • Event Details -  The conference dates, topic, the conference program, the subject and the abstract (a draft is applicable as well) of the lecture the speaker will participate in.   
    • In the case of a daylong seminar, please provide the details of the daylong seminar, and the role of the visitor in that seminar.     
  3. A recommendation of the faculty dean or the head of the department for supporting this speaker's participation - sent according to the guidelines described here  
  4. To complete the submission process go to the Submission Status page and follow the guidelines

 

Please note: Following the Halbert Centre's approval of the grant

  1. Clearly refer to the Halbert Centre's support for funding the guest's talk, in the relevant publications.
  2. At the end of the conference/lecture/seminar, the project managers are required to write a report summarizing the activity and send it to the Halbert Centre, to be distributed to the academic committee members in Israel and members of the advisory committee in Canada
