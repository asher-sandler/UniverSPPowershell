POSTDOCTORAL FELLOWSHIPS AT 

THE TRUMAN INSTITUTE

FOR THE ACADEMIC YEAR 2021-2022
 

The Institute is offering:

  • ​Truman Institute Postdoctoral Fellowships (up to 2 years)
  • The Maurice J. and Fay B. Karpf Peace Prize Awards (1 year)

​in the amount of 80,000 shekels per year for research connected to the study of peace and/or conflict resolution.  Priority will be given to research in the following areas: Middle East; The Israeli-Palestinian Conflict; Jerusalem.


Only candidates who received their PhD after September 2018 may apply.

The Institute's academic committee will determine the allocation of the various fellowships and prizes. Applicants will be asked to specify whether they are applying for one or two years.

Successful candidates must be present at the Truman Institute at least three days per week and participate in the doctoral seminar and other activities of the Institute.

 

Closing date for applications: February 16, 2021

 

Submission Procedure:

A. Complete and submit application form (personal information questionnaire)
B. Upload the following documents to your personal folder (see instructions for uploading):

  1. PhD certificate (or proof that the dissertation has been submitted)

  2. CV and a list of academic publications

  3. Short description of the research to be carried out at the Institute (up to 3 pages double spaced)

  4. Prospective candidates must establish contact with the relevant Faculty member willing to sponsor the application. A sponsor must have the rank of Lecturer (or above) and he/she must be on the Regular Academic Track at the Hebrew University – please upload a letter from the prospective sponsor

C. Two letters of recommendation should be se​​nt directly to the email address provided by the system (see instructions)
D. Please follow the instructions shown on 'Submission Status' page
 

Rights and Duties:

Successful applicants will be required to sign a form confirming their agreement to the rights and duties of Truman Institute Fellows.

  1. To submit a short scientific progress report and an updated list of publications and presentations at the end of the fellowship year.

  2. In cases where the fellowship is for a period of one year with an option for further years, renewal of the fellowship is at the discretion of the Academic Committee at the end of each year.  A detailed research report and a letter from the dissertation advisor or faculty host must be submitted to the Academic Committee upon request.

  3. To mention the affiliation with the Truman Institute in every printed or digital publication that was prepared, partly or fully, during the research year (even if it was sent for publication after the research year).

  4. To mention the Truman Institute as your affiliation in all conferences, here and abroad, and in all interviews and publications in the media.

  5. To be present at the Institute at least three days per week in the course of the school year, other than planned absences of which the management of the Institute should be informed in advance.

  6. Participation in all fellow-oriented Truman activities. In addition, active participation in the ongoing academic activity of the Institute, including conferences, seminars, etc., is encouraged. Doctoral students and post docs are expected to initiate and organize academic activities during their stay at the institute.

  7. The fellowships come with restrictions on the number of hours which can be worked in paid employment, as specified in the rules of HU's Academic Secretariat.

  8. To participate and assist in hosting and in meetings with guests of the Institute from Israel and abroad.

  9. To lecture on the subject of your research in various forums that the Institute shall organize.

  10. Fellows are entitled to photocopying and printing services at the Institute, up to 1,000 pages per year (we will appreciate efforts to use less paper as part of overall environmental awareness).

  11. Fellows will be given office space at the Institute which they will share with other fellows.

  12. Fellows are entitled to use, when possible, the event halls at the Institute.

  13. The facilities and services of the Institute shall not be used other than according to the terms and conditions established in advance.

  14. The Academic Committee is authorized to terminate the activity of a fellow at its discretion.

 

For further information:

Please email the Truman Institute at truman@savion.huji.ac.il or call 972-2-5882300
