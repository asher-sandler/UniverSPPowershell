Postdoctoral Fellowships at  

The Truman Institute 

for the Academic Year 2022-2023
 

The Institute is offering postdoctoral fellowships (up to two years), the William A. Brown Endowed Scholarship and the Maurice J. and Fay B. Karpf Peace Prize Awards (one year) ​in the amount of 80,000 shekels per year for each fellowship/prize.

The fellowships are intended for research on peace and/or conflict resolution.  Priority will be given to research on the Middle East, the Israeli-Palestinian Conflict and Jerusalem.


Applicants must have received their PhD after September 2019.


The Institute's academic committee will determine the allocation of the various fellowships and prizes. Applicants will be asked to specify whether they are applying for one or two years.

Successful candidates will be expected to be present at the Truman Institute at least three days per week and to participate in the various activities of the Institute, including the Institute's research seminar.

 

DEADLINE FOR APPLICATIONS: FEBRUARY 7, 2022

 

Submission Procedure:

A. Complete and submit application form (personal information questionnaire)
B. The following documents should be uploaded via the Documents Upload page:

  1. Cover letter with a description of the plans for the period of the scholarship and their relevance to the Truman Institute (up to 2 pages, in English).

  2. CV and a list of academic publications - listing separately work in progress and work accepted for publication, noting the impact factor of the journal in which the work(s) was/were published.
  3. PhD certificate (or proof that the dissertation has been submitted).
  4. Prospective candidates must establish contact with the relevant Faculty member willing to sponsor the application. A sponsor must have the rank of Lecturer (or above) and he/she must be on the Regular Academic Track at the Hebrew University – please upload a letter from the prospective sponsor.
C. Two letters of recommendation should be se​​nt directly to the email address provided by the system (see instructions)
D. Please follow the instructions shown on 'Submission Status' page
 
Rights and Duties:

Successful applicants will be required to sign a form confirming their agreement to the rights and duties of Truman Institute Fellows.

 I undertake the following:

  1. During the period of eligibility for the Truman Institute Scholarship, I will not be permitted to work in any job, whether paid or unpaid, in order to allow me as a scholarship recipient to advance in my research.  I am aware that according to the regulations for postdoctoral fellows ("post-doctoral") on the website of the Academic Administration Division of the University, it is possible to obtain a permit to work for a limited number of teaching hours (full details in the regulations).

  2. I know that in the event that I cease my research, my eligibility for a scholarship will cease.

  3. I am aware that the Truman Institute is entitled to stop payment of the scholarship at any time if, at the discretion of the Institute's Academic Committee, progress in research work is unsatisfactory, or academic achievement does not justify continued support, or that the terms of the scholarship have been breached. This is after I have been given the opportunity to make my claims in writing or orally. This decision can be appealed to the Vice-Rector.

  4. I  will be required to submit a short scientific progress report and an updated list of publications and presentations at the end of the scholarship year.

  5. In cases where the scholarship is for a period of one year with an option for additional years, renewal of the scholarship is at the discretion of the Academic Committee at the end of each year. A detailed research report from the fellow and a letter from the dissertation advisor or faculty host must be submitted to the Academic Committee upon request.

  6. I am aware that in cases where it is necessary to travel overseas for a long period (a semester or longer) during the scholarship period, prior approval must be requested from the Academic Committee. The application will include:

    • An explanation of the importance and the contribution of a stay abroad to the research;

    • Approval of the advisor, including an explanation of how the relationship with the student will be maintained during the trip;

    • Confirmation from the host institution or from the host researcher (if there is a formal host).

      The Scholarship Committee of the Institute will consider the application but does not commit to approve it. As a general rule, travel for a period more than 12 months will not be approved.

  7. I undertake that most of my academic activities will be at the Truman Institute on the university campus. I am aware that I must obtain prior approval from the Academic Committee for ongoing academic activity outside the University.

  8. To mention the affiliation with the Truman Institute in every printed or digital publication that was prepared, in part or in full, during the research period funded by the Truman Institute (even if it was sent for publication after the research period).

  9. To mention the Truman Institute as my affiliation in all conferences, in Israel and abroad, and in all interviews and publications in the media.

  10. To be present at the Institute at least three days per week in the course of the school year, not taking into consideration planned absences of which the management of the Institute must be informed in advance.

  11. To participate in all fellow-oriented Truman activities. In addition, active participation in the ongoing academic activities of the Institute, including conferences, seminars, etc. Doctoral students and post-doctoral students are expected to initiate and organize academic activities during their stay at the institute.

  12. To participate and assist in hosting and meetings with the guests of the Institute from Israel and abroad.

  13. To lecture on the subject of your research in various forums that the Institute shall organize.

  14. Fellows are entitled to photocopying and printing services at the Institute, up to 1,000 pages per year (we will appreciate efforts to use less paper as part of overall environmental awareness).

  15. Fellows will be given office space at the Institute which they will share with other fellows.

  16. Fellows are entitled to use, when available, the event halls at the Institute.

  17. The facilities and services of the Institute shall not be used other than according to the terms and conditions established in advance.


For further information:

Please email the Truman Institute at truman@savion.huji.ac.il or call 972-2-5882300
