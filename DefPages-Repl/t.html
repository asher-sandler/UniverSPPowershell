Application stages:

The first stage evaluates your eligibility for the International MSc Programs based on the Hebrew University's recognition of your university, your academic background and final grade point average (GPA). If you are eligible, your application will continue through the application process. If you are not eligible, your application will be concluded. We will notify you of either status within about three weeks of receiving your application.

Note: If you are eligible for the program and continue with the application, you will be required to: a) pay the application fee (see section Application Fees below), and b) arrange for your official transcript to be sent directly from the registrar of your university to our office, preferably by email or online

The second stage is the continuation of your application. The MSc Academic Committee will review all applications in the Spring and interview semi-final candidates on Skype or Zoom to select the final candidates. Final results fall into four categories: 1) accepted and granted a scholarship; 2) accepted and wait-listed for a scholarship; 3) accepted (no scholarship); or 4) not accepted. We will notify you by email within about three weeks of the final decision.

Application fees​​​:

The application fee is required after we notify you that you are eligible for the program. If you do not pay the application fee, the application will be discontinued. The application fee must be after bank commission deductions (in the case of a bank transfer).

Application fee: $100 (after bank fees)

 

​Enrollment fees:​​

The enrollment fee is required if you have been accepted to the program. The enrollment fee must be after bank commission deductions (in the case of a bank transfer).​

  • Scholarship students: A $500 deposit (after bank fees) is required from all applicants who are offered and accept a scholarship from the International School, as well as from all international students (non-Israeli citizens). The deposit is a requirement of the Ministry of Interior. The deposit will be refunded to the payer or student upon graduation from the Faculty of Agriculture and departure from Israel.

  • Self-funding students: A $1,000 tuition advance is required from all students paying tuition. The amount will be deducted from the full tuition.

Scholar​ships:​​

The International School of Agricultural Sciences offers a limited number of partial and full scholarships only to selected applicants from developing countries. Partial scholarships may cover tuition and/or other expenses. Full scholarships cover tuition, a shared dormitory room on campus, a monthly living stipend, medical insurance and an entry visa. The scholarship does not cover round-trip airfare or initial living expenses. Applicants must have sufficient funds for these expenses.

Selection is highly competitive. We strongly recommend that you seek other funding sources prior or parallel to applying to our programs. There is a partial list of scholarships on our website: http://intschool.agri.huji.ac.il/schol-sources . We also encourage you to approach your country's government and research the Internet for scholarships relevant to your interests.​
