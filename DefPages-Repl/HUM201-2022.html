The Roger and Susan Hertog Center for the Archaeological Study of Jerusalem and Judah-2024

 

The Hertog Center invites scholars and graduate students whose research pertains to all aspects of the archaeology of Jerusalem and Judea to submit applications for supporting research, excavations, and publications

Please Note: To complete the process, you must go to the 'Submission Status' page and follow the instructions

Research grants will be awarded in the amount of $1,000-$10,000 per project, and for graduate students up to $2,000

Support will be provided on the basis of academic excellence.
Eligible to apply for a grant:
• Graduate or postgraduate research students enrolled in the Department of Archaeology and the Ancient Near East at The Hebrew University of Jerusalem whose research deals with the archeology of Jerusalem or Judea (in all periods)
• Researchers at the Institute of Archaeology of The Hebrew University of Jerusalem conducting archaeological studies relating to Jerusalem or Judea
• External scholars who conduct research related to the study and publication of findings from the excavations conducted by the Institute of Archaeology at The Hebrew University or such that are the responsibility of the Institute and involve the archaeology of Jerusalem or Judea
The regulations can be found on the Institute's website here.
 

Commitment to Submit a Research Report and Accreditation

For research proposals that are awarded a grant, the applicant is obligated to submit a detailed summary report to the Hertog Center by September 30 of the grant year.
The name of the Hertog Center must be cited in full in any publication or lecture presenting the research fruits using the following wording (or its exact translation):
This study was supported by the Roger and Susan Hertog Center for the Archeological study of Jerusalem and Judea
 

Application deadline:  20.08.2023

The submission is made by the submitter’s approval at the end


Application procedure

1. Fill out the online application form.

2. Upload the following documents via the Documents Upload page

 

 CV of the applicant and / or the research partner, unless the applicant is a permanent member of the institute's staff.
Make sure your resume includes the following details:
◦ Academic degree of the applicant and his rank and the name of the institution where he works / studies.
◦ Details about education and academic degrees
◦ In cases of joint research the details of the partner researcher should be added:

  •  Academic degree of the applicant and the name of the affiliated institution.
  • Relevant skills of the applicant  and the co-researcher (e.g., knowledge of languages ​​or software required to carry out the research, etc.).

List of publications of the applicant and the partner researchers, starting with the latest publications. The publications related to the requested research topic should be marked with an asterisk

Description of the study: Describe in detail the proposed study (up to 6 pages). Present the main issues that the proposed research addresses and the expected contribution, citing relevant publications. The description should discuss the methodology involved. If the proposed research includes fieldwork, a map / plan must be attached in which the size of the excavation area and its location on the site can be seen.

Only graduate and postgraduate students are required to add:

Moderator Approval: It is mandatory to attach a signed certificate from the facilitator who passed the proposal

3. One Letter of recommendations,  submitted according to this procedure.

4. To complete the process, please follow the instructions in the Application Submittal Status. 

 

 


 

 

 

For rules and regulations please see http://archaeology.huji.ac.il
