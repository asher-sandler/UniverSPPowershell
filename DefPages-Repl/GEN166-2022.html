The JBC Scholarship for Undergraduates

 in Brain & Cognitive Sciences

The Jerusalem Brain Community (JBC) sees great importance in promoting academic excellence and fostering future generations of brain researchers at the Hebrew University. The JBC Undergraduate Scholarship enhances educational goals and promotes the continuation of studies towards a Master's Degree in brain research, at the Hebrew University. The Scholarship will be awarded to outstanding and gifted undergraduate students in their last year of studies, who are interested in furthering their research using a multi-disciplinary approach to brain sciences. The scholarship provides a unique opportunity for students who are at the beginning of their scientific and professional careers, to devote themselves exclusively to their course work, develop their research skills, maintain academic excellence and finish their degree in a timely manner. The JBC scholarship encourages students to broaden their exposure and increase their scientific interests by conducting research in a lab of a JBC member.

Amount: $4,000 (2200 NIS per month)

We offer a six-month fellowship in the second semester of the 3rd year (towards a BA or BSc degree).

The fellowship is offered from May 1, 2021 through October 30, 2021.

Eligibility:

  • 3rd year Undergraduate students, with a clear interest in brain & cognitive science
  • Average grade: 90 or above (or at the top 10% of the class)
  • Applications for JBC fellowships are to students conducting research in a laboratory of a JBC member

Scholarship Requirements:

  • Play an active role in JBC community.
  • Participate in JBC activities, e.g., attend JBC-sponsored events, etc.
  • Update the JBC on your experience, the fruits of your work, and the insights gained.
  • Acknowledge JBC in publications and presentations.

 

Application Process:

A. Please fill out the Online Application form
 
B. Please upload the following documents according to the upload instructions:

  • Photograph

  • Copy of Academic Standing from the university (grades and year).
  • A research proposal (limited to 1 page)
C. A recommendation letter from a JBC Member, expressing his/her approval to conduct research in their Lab- sent according to the guidelines described here
 
D. Please follow the instructions shown on the 'Submission Status' page


Only complete applications will be considered

Last date to submit applications – April 3, 2021
