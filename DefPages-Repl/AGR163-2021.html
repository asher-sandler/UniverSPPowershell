Appleby Canada Scholarships for 2nd and 3rd year BSc Students – 2021-2022

The Robert H. Smith Faculty of Agriculture, Food and Environment and the Keren Kayemet LeIsrael (KKL, the Jewish National Fund) announce the scholarship program for the academic year 2021-2022, funded by the KKL, for 2nd and 3rd year BSc students.

The program aims to encourage outstanding students to study and excel in the Nature Conservation and Land Management Program.

Students in their 2nd or 3rd year in the Nature Conservation and Land Management Program, with a grade average of no less than 80, may apply for the scholarship.

The scholarship for BSc students is 6,000 NIS.

The recipients will be required to attend several events:

  • The Appleby Scholarship awards ceremony in the Faculty of Agriculture
  • The KKL annual scholarship awards ceremony
  • Exposition day to the work done in KKL and to forest and land management in Israel.

Students may apply even if they receive other scholarships or awards for academic excellence (such as the rector's list, the dean's list or other awards from internal or external sources), provided that the overall sum of their scholarships is no higher than a full tuition.

Application deadline: November 28th, 2021

Application procedure

  1. Fill out the online application form.
  2. Upload the following documents to the Personal Document Upload File according to Documents Upload page
    • Confirmation of enrollment for 2021-2022
    • CV, concisely written according to Documents Upload page/font>
    • Record of studies showing:
      • Enrollment to the Nature Conservation and Land Management Program
      • Courses for the previous year(s) (1st for 2nd year students, 1st and 2nd year for 3rd year students)
      • Full program for the current year (2nd or 3rd year, respectively)
  3. To complete the process, please follow the instructions in the Application Submittal Status.

Please verify that the online form is fully and precisely filled out. Applications submitted without a fully completed online form will be disqualified. Applications submitted without all the necessary data (recommendations or any other required documents) will be disqualified. All the required documents must be uploaded until the application deadline. Documents submitted after the deadline will not be considered.
