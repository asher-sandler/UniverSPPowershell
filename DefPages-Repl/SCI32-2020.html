The Department of Applied Physics

Tenure Track Faculty Positions - 2020-2021

 

The Applied Physics Department is expanding and invites applicants for multiple junior (tenure-track) and senior (tenured) faculty positions in the broadly defined fields encapsulated by electrical/electronic engineering and applied science. The department is especially interested, but not limited, in candidates investigating (i) electron devices and circuits (including VLSI design and architecture), (ii) devices and systems for quantum computing, information and sensing, (iii) energy conversion, harvesting, and storage, and (iv) RF, electromagnetics and 5G communications. Priority, however, will be given to the overall originality and promise of the candidate's work over any specific area of specialization.

Successful candidates will be expected to establish and sustain an outstanding research program and must have excellent ability to teach effectively at both the undergraduate and graduate levels. Successful candidates will have a relevant doctorate or equivalent experience and an outstanding record of achievement and impactful research (in academic, industry and/or at national laboratories). The department encourages women and underrepresented minority candidates to apply.

 

Deadline for applications: December 4th, 2020

 

Application Submission

Applications should be written in English and include the following:

A. Please fill out the Online Application Form

B. Please upload the following documents according to the upload instructions:

  • Curriculum Vitae

  • List of publications

  • Statement of research

  • Teaching interests

  • Copies of 1-3 representative papers

C. Recommendation letters from advisors (Ph.D. and postdoctoral) - sent according to the guidelines described here.

D. Please follow the instructions shown on 'Submission Status' page

 

Candidates are strongly encouraged to submit their application before November 6, 2020, which is when the department hiring discussions are scheduled to begin. Applications will be accepted until the deadline of December 4, 2020.  Candidates who pass the first review will be invited to the department to give a seminar (a.k.a. "job talk") and meet with the department members.
